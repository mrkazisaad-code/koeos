**Headline:**
This is not just an AI shift. It is a shift in how firms create value.

**Body:**
For the first time, software creation, knowledge work, and decision support can be produced at near-zero marginal cost. That changes the economics of the enterprise.

Between model access and business value, there is an infrastructure gap.

Enterprises have access to models, tools, and implementation partners. What they do not have is the operating layer that converts that access into production systems that are owned, governed, and commercially defensible.

AI Commercialization Infrastructure is that layer. It sits between AI capability and business value — and it is where conversion either happens or fails.

## PAGE 5: SECTORS

**Purpose:** Organize vertical relevance. Short, sharp. No empty industry pages.

**URL:** /sectors

---

Five sector blocks. Each one follows the same structure: why the sector is exposed → what conversion failure looks like → what architecture is required → one-line engagement hook.

---

### Defence & Sovereign Systems

**Why exposed:** AI deployment in defence is also a sovereignty question. Platform dependency, supply chain coercion, and foreign control risk shape whether AI can be trusted in core environments. Capability conversion — not just capability access — determines operational advantage.

**What failure looks like:** Pilot demonstrations that never reach operational use. Technology investments stranded between demonstration and procurement. No structured pathway from vendor capability to sovereign operating deployment.

**What is required:** Sovereign deployment architecture. Operational testing and readiness frameworks. Integration and procurement pathway design. Capability conversion discipline.

---

### Government & Public Sector

**Why exposed:** Public sector AI carries accountability requirements that most deployment models are not designed to meet. Citizen-facing AI must be evidenced, governed, and defensible — not just functional.

**What failure looks like:** AI tools deployed without audit trails. Citizen-impacting decisions made by systems with no evidence framework. Regulatory exposure accumulating silently.

**What is required:** Accountability architecture. Evidence and assurance frameworks. Production systems designed for public scrutiny and regulatory compliance.

---

### Regulated Enterprise (Banking, Insurance, Legal)

**Why exposed:** Regulated industries face converging pressure from AI capability expansion and tightening supervisory expectations. EU AI Act, DORA, and sector-specific requirements demand that AI is not just deployed but governed and commercially accountable.

**What failure looks like:** AI in production without regulatory evidence. Compliance gaps between what is reported and what is operating. Client-facing outputs that cannot be fully defended.

**What is required:** Compliance-grade production architecture. Regulatory evidence frameworks. Operating model redesign for AI under supervisory pressure.

---

### Professional Services & Knowledge Work

**Why exposed:** AI is compressing the cost structure of knowledge work faster than most firms are redesigning their operating models. The firms that convert AI into workflow redesign and commercial leverage will outperform. The firms that treat it as a productivity tool will face margin compression.

**What failure looks like:** AI tools deployed with no workflow redesign. Productivity gains that do not translate into commercial value. Pricing models unchanged despite fundamentally different cost structures.

**What is required:** Workflow redesign architecture. Commercial model transformation. Operating model reset for AI-native knowledge work.

---

### Critical Infrastructure

**Why exposed:** Energy, transport, telecommunications, and health systems operate in environments where AI failure has systemic consequence. Trust, control, and evidence requirements are non-negotiable.

**What failure looks like:** AI in operational systems with no verification layer. Decision-support tools that cannot be audited. Deployment without operational stress testing.

**What is required:** High-assurance deployment architecture. Verification and evidence systems. Operational testing before scale.

## KEY COPY DECISIONS

**"Diffuse" is removed everywhere.** Replaced with "convert," "absorb," or "reach" depending on context. The concept of diffusion is preserved in the thesis (operating models were not built for this shift) without the academic vocabulary.

**Venture building sits under the core category.** It appears on the homepage only as part of the sectors row (Defence & Sovereign Systems) and in the engagement models. It gets its own page (/ventures) but is never positioned at the same level as the primary consulting offer.

**No framework names in the hero.** PCI, Aegir, and the Exposure Assessment appear in Section 5 of the homepage and on /the-model — not in the first thing a visitor sees. The category comes first. The systems come after the visitor understands why they matter.

**Engagement models are explicit.** Buyers need to understand how to buy you. The four-type progression (Diagnostic → Reset → Architecture → Embedded) gives them a mental model for engagement without requiring a sales conversation first.

**The site has 7 pages, not 12.** Every page has real content. No thin industry pages. No blog with two posts. Insights gets added when there are at least 4 published pieces worth reading.

---

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- Do not lead with "AI strategy" language — you are not a strategy firm
- Do not make venture building equal to the core category on the homepage
- Do not dump every framework on the homepage — pick 3
- Do not copy deck paragraphs into the website — the deck is sequential, the site is modular
- Do not create a blog section with nothing in it
- Do not use "diffuse" or "diffusion" as a verb in visitor-facing copy
- Do not add team members who are not active principals
- Do not use stock photography — dark backgrounds, architecture diagrams, data visualization
- Do not build sector pages until there is real content for each one — use tiles on the homepage until then
